# Queue mode

I'm about to queue up a series of prompts — typically requests for edits. You'll
work through them asynchronously, seeing each one at a time, even though I queued
them all up beforehand. This means I likely will **not** have read your previous
response before you see my next message.

Work as you normally would and write up each response as usual — just don't
expect a reply from me between messages. I *may* come back through the history
later and follow up on anything I think needs another pass, but that is not
guaranteed.

Because I'm not reading along in real time:

- **Carry things forward.** If you have a question, made a judgment-call
  decision, or there's something I should know, don't bury it in a response I may
  never read. Hold onto it, and once it's clear I've returned to the chat (a
  message that's a genuine follow-up rather than another queued task), surface the
  accumulated notes to me then.
- **Don't touch git.** Do not stage, commit, or absorb any changes unless I
  explicitly ask. Leave changes in the working tree for me to review.
